Each of these is a short architecture decision record: the choice made, the
alternative(s) seriously considered, and why the tradeoff went the way it
did. They're written from the actual design specs and implementation plans
in the repo, not reconstructed after the fact.

- **[Raft over Paxos](/docs/decisions/raft-over-paxos)** — why consensus is
  Raft, not Paxos or a Paxos variant.
- **[Pre-vote and read-index](/docs/decisions/pre-vote-and-read-index)** —
  why elections use a non-disruptive pre-vote round and reads use
  read-index, plus a real safety bug the design caught before merge.
- **[LSM over B-tree](/docs/decisions/lsm-over-btree)** — why the storage
  engine is log-structured, and why the Raft log store is a separate
  component rather than another LSM consumer.
- **[Atomic flush via temp+rename](/docs/decisions/atomic-flush)** — how a
  memtable flush or compaction avoids ever exposing a partially-written
  SSTable to a reader.
- **[Seqno recovery](/docs/decisions/seqno-recovery)** — why sequence
  numbers must recover from SSTables, not just the WAL, on restart.
